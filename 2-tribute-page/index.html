Dr. Carl Sagan

The astronomer who brought the inspired wonder of the cosmos into your home

A brief timeline of Carl Sagan's life and accomplishments
1934 Born in Brooklyn, New York 1951 Graduated valedictorian of Rahway Highschool at 16. Started attending University of Chicago. 1954 Awarded Bachelor of Arts 1955 Awarded Bachelor of Science in Physics 1956 Earned Masters of Science in Physics 1958 Worked on Project A119 - a secret United States Air Force plan to detonate a nuclear warhead on the moon. In 1999 it was found that Sagan had applied to UC Berkley using two titles of classified papers from Project A119. 1960 Earned Doctor of Philosophy in astronomy and astrophysics. Started a 2 year Miller Fellowship at UC Berkley. 1963 Worked as an assistant professor at Harvard and worked at Smithsonian Astrophysical Observatory. 1968 Denied tenureship at Harvard unexpectedly, in part due to recommendations by former colleagues who didn't like his publicized science advocacy work (perceived as self promotion on the backs of other's work). He then moved to work at the Cornell astronomy department for the rest of his life. 1972 Assembled the first physical message sent into space: a plaque attached to space probe Pioneer 10 1977 Wrote "The Dragons of Eden: Speculations on the Evolution of Human Intelligence" 1980 Wrote and narrated "Cosmos: A Personal Voyage", a 13 part science themed variety show to inspire a general audience of viewers, whom Sagan felt had lost interest in science, partly due to a stifled educational system. 1985 Wrote "Contact", a hard science fiction novel about the first contact between humans and extra-terrestrials. 1996 Passed away at 62 years of age, from pneumonia after enduring myelodysplastia for 2 years.
